### Takeaways 
- You need to have the guts to be a startup founder, train your guts by asking questions in class 
	- Practice questioning experts and successful people. Learn to challenge the status quo. 
- Over-reliance on AI makes you think less 
- Invention is something new, innovation is incremental change
- Have different profit models 
	- e.g. netflix - pay-per-rental to subscription model
	- freemium model -> dropbox 
	- ad-supported freemium model -> spotify 
- Network: leveraging connections to create value 
	- uber: vast network of drivers and riders 
	- notion: flexible shared documents
- Process: improving how things are down 
	- stripe: made integrating online payments simple 
	- spacex: pioneered use of reusable rockets to reduce time and cost of manufacturing 
	- amazon: pioneered use of robotics and automation in fulfilment centers 
- product system
	- apple: strong ecosystem of apps and connected devices

### Book: Unlocking the Customer Value Chain (CVC)
- Startups don't steal customers from incumbents, they steal activities
	- they steal activities that customers don't enjoy the experience of 
	- e.g. physically turning up for class 
- If you can map out the customer value chain, you can identify the activities that you can steal 
	- After mapping out the activities, label them according to: 
		- Value-capturing -> reduce friction
		- Value-creating -> make it 10x better
		- Value-eroding -> reduce / eliminate
