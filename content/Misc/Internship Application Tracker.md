# Simular 
Link: https://www.simular.ai 
### Job Description: 
**What you’ll do**

As a Software Engineer Intern at Simular, you’ll work closely with our founding team to help bring AI agents into the real world. Depending on your skills and interests, you may:

- Contribute to core product development, building new features and improving performance.
- Work with the infrastructure team on scaling systems and virtual machines.
- Support Forward Deployed Engineering projects, writing scripts and integrations to adapt agents to customer needs.
- Collaborate with designers and researchers to prototype new ideas and test them with users.
- Gain hands-on experience in a fast-moving startup where every line of code can make an impact.

**You might be a fit if**

- Currently pursuing a Bachelor’s or Master’s degree in Computer Science, Computer Engineering, Information Systems, or a related technical field.
- Solid programming skills in Python, JavaScript/TypeScript, or a similar language.
- Strong CS fundamentals and problem-solving ability; competitive programming background (e.g. ACM ICPC, IOI, Google Code Jam, or equivalent contests) is highly preferred.
- Willingness to dive into different parts of the stack, from frontend to backend to deployment scripts.
- Curiosity, adaptability, and eagerness to learn in an ambiguous, fast-paced environment.
- Strong communication and collaboration skills.
- Bonus: prior internship or project experience in AI, agents, or productivity tools.

### What makes you particularly well suited for this role?
What makes me a strong fit for Simular is my ability to bridge high-level system design with deep, fundamental programming rigor. I believe this is a combination essential for scaling AI agents in an ambiguous startup environment.

First, I bring proven experience in infrastructure and scalability. Having earned my AWS Certified Solutions Architect Associate credential, I’ve moved beyond theory to practical application. During my previous internship, I designed and implemented a highly available, scalable infrastructure. This directly aligns with Simular’s need to scale virtual machines and systems as your agent deployments grow.

Second, I possess exceptional CS fundamentals. As a Student Tutor for CS2030 (Programming Methodology II), I don’t just write code; I mentor others on object-oriented design, functional programming, and software patterns. This role requires a level of technical mastery that allows me to deconstruct complex problems and learn new stacks rapidly. Whether it’s diving into your core product or writing custom integrations for customers, I have the theoretical backbone to ensure my code is efficient and maintainable.

Finally, I am drawn to Simular’s mission. I thrive in environments where code has an immediate impact, and I am eager to apply my background in scalable systems to help bring the next generation of AI agents into the real world.
