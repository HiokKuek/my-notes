# Factors that drive Technological Innovation  Racing 
### First Mover's Advantage 
 Motivate race to acquire customers before competitors enter 
- increasing returns to scale & switching costs 
- preemptions of scarce resources (e.g. long-term ontracts of raw materials, skilled labor, channel partnerships, government licence)
- Preemption of Key Patents (e.g. Philips with compact disc technology licensing)

### Late Mover Advantages 
- Reduce R&D costs through reverse engineering 
- Leapfrog leaders with newly invented, superior technology
- Free ride on first mover's investments in educating customres 
- avoid pioneers' positioning errors on customer needs and value proposition (time & money)


### Fat Cat vs Lean Cat
- Fat Cat: refuse to move, battle to acquire new customers 
- Lean Cat: will battle, to acquire new customers 

| **Barriers to Entry** ↓ / **Pace of Change** →   | **Slow / Stable Industry**                                                                                                                                              | **Fast / Disruptive Industry**                                                                                                                                             |
| ------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **High Barriers** (hard for new rivals to enter) | **🐱 Fat Cat** • Extract rents, lobby regulators, buy rivals. • Focus on cash flow + efficiency, not risky innovation. • Examples: Utilities, telecoms, oil majors.     | **Mixed Strategy** • You’re protected, but disruption could still bite. • Stay mostly fat, but invest in R&D or acquire disruptors early. • Examples: Banking, big pharma. |
| **Low Barriers** (easy for new rivals to enter)  | **Defensive Lean Cat** • Industry is slow, but rivals can copy you. • Differentiate with brand, service, or cost leadership. • Examples: Retail chains, consumer goods. | **🐈 Lean Cat** • Must stay hungry and agile. • Constant innovation, fast product cycles, strong culture. • Examples: Tech startups, e-commerce, consumer apps.            |


### Over-investing 
Why some firms over-invest, relative to long-term profit 
1. Overconfidence Biases -> underestimate risks 
	 - Perceive themselves to be in control of the situation 
	 - Emotionally Invested 
 2. Speculative Bubbles 
 3. Internal control and compensation systems fail to prevent managers from pursuing personal priorities, at the expense of long term return to shareholders

### Under-investing 
1. Antirust constraints 
2. Funding constraints 
3. Planning & Budgeting Processes
