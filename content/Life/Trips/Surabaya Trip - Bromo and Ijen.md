I booked a trip to Surabaya during reading week of Y2S2 and here is everything that went down. 

### A few days before the trip 
It has been a really busy semester for me. If you were to look at my calendar I am quite sure most of my days were packed. I saw that I would be quite free during reading week and decided that I had to go somewhere. My philosophy was that I may not have the time to travel in the future and I felt that I had sufficient time to study for my finals after returning from the trip. I have been wanting to go for hikes and started my research on the mountains surrounding Singapore. 

I found a few feasible options: 
- Mount Agung in Bali, Indonesia
- Mount Kinabalu in Sabah, Malaysia 
- Mount Bromo and Mount Ijen in East Java, Indonesia 

I picked Mount Bromo and Mount Ijen in the end because they had the cheapest flight tickets and I got to climb two mountains instead of one. I also found that it was cheaper for me to fly out from Senai Airport (Malaysia) which meant that I could sneak in a morning trip to JB and visit my grandmother right before I fly off to Surabaya. The trip was ONN! 

### Day 1: Flight from Senai -> Surabaya
The flight was an evening flight that was 2 hours long. I woke up at around 8am and made my way to JB where I had Kaya Toast and eggs with my Grandmother. 
 ![[photo_2026-05-06 22.46.28.jpeg | Breakfast at Soon Cafe | 300]]
 I then made my way to Senai airport for my 5pm flight. The flight was 2 hours long but Surabaya is 1 hour behind Singapore which meant that my touchdown time was 6pm at Surabaya. My accommodation was located near Wonokromo which was where I would be going the next day to catch a train towards Probolinggo. I was quite tired after the flight and found myself a simple dinner before calling it a day. 

### Day 2: Surabaya -> Probolinggo -> Cemoro Lawang 
Cemoro Lawang was the base camp for Mount Bromo. The most direct route from Surabaya to Cemoro Lawang was 114km but it meant that you would have to hire a private driver. Since I was in no rush for time, I decided to make my way there using the public transport that was available to me. I thought it was also a good opportunity to experience the 'local life' as well. 

I woke up at 5am and booked a grab bike to Wonokromo (train station at Surabaya). Even though the train station was only 1km away, I've learnt that Surabaya is not walkable. There were no proper pavements and the situation on the road was quite messy. I was at Wonokromo before 6 and managed to catch the sunrise while I was there! 

![[photo_2026-05-07 18.06.07.jpeg | Sunrise at Wonokromo! | 300]] 

2 hours later, I arrived at Probolinggo. This was when the journey started getting interesting. There was a small shuttle bus that brought us from Probolinggo to Cemoro Lawang. However, the bus would only depart if there were sufficient passengers. While waiting for more passengers, I had some locally cooked food and also made friends with a fellow traveller. I learnt that he was previously at bali and actually took a sleeper bus the night before. The sleeper bus was supposed to drop him off at Probolinggo but it didn't  😂.  He actually spent the morning on another bus to get to Probolinggo!
